About

I work at the intersection of technology, education, and writing. My career has spanned big tech, national nonprofits, and community organizations—always focused on who gets displaced when new systems arrive, and who gets a say in how they're built.

I write short fiction exploring interiority, grace, and machines. My literary interests center on writers who move between worlds: Zadie Smith, Toni Morrison, Zora Neale Hurston, George Eliot, Montaigne, Ray Bradbury, George Orwell.

I identify as split between being an empathetic poet and an operations professional, and see integrating these as central to my work and identity.
